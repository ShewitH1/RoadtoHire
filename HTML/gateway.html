Professional Development:

Through the Gateway experience, I discovered how important personal branding is. Beyond merely my LinkedIn profile and resume, my professional image influences how colleagues and employers view me. I've become more deliberate in my work contacts as a result of realizing this, concentrating on how I show myself, stand up for my demands, and manage my time. This realization motivates me to improve the way I highlight my abilities and experiences so they fit the polished image I like to present.

Technical Development:

From a technical perspective, I came to understand the significance of responsive design. Though I'm still developing my skills, I've realized that in today's digital world, having websites that adapt to many screen sizes without any trouble at all is crucial. My approach to coding has changed as a result of this discovery, and I now emphasize the importance of keeping the end user in mind when making decisions. Knowing that it will greatly improve user experience and show a greater understanding of web development, I'm committed to honing this talent.

Personal Development:

For me, the Gateway experience brought home the value of advocating for oneself. I came to understand that in order to succeed, I must actively look for openings and make requests. Self-advocacy, whether it takes the form of asking for criticism, looking for mentorship, or making sure one's voice is heard in group situations, is essential for both professional and personal development. This has changed my perspective from waiting for possibilities to actively seeking them out, which I think will be really helpful as I move on with my journey toward the final interview and eventually the tech business.
